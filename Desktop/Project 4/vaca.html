D.C.

Washington

One of the most accessible city in the USA for both wheelchair users and people with other physical disabilities. The nation's capital sets a high standard in the word accessibility around lays down a perfect example for the rest of the world.Even if you don’t plan on staying long, there are several accessible restaurants, malls, parks, and restrooms.

Chicago

Illinois

As America’s third-largest city, Chi-town has a lot of accessible neighborhoods and destinations across the city. There are a lot of affordable accommodations for the disabled and wheelchair adapted buses and taxis. Then there are Open Taxes. Open Taxis is a centralized dispatch service that operates around the clock to provide wheelchair accessible vehicles.

Los Angeles

California

Los Angeles is one of the most accessible cities in the USA for people that rely on wheelchair and scooters for mobility.You can easily get around in the city, even if you’re stuck in a wheelchair. Every metro rail station in LA is wheelchair friendly, you can enter and exit trains on your own. The standard of living is just above average and there are lots of wheelchair-friendly parks, stadiums, and neighborhoods across the city

Albuquerque

New Mexico

Albuquerque is perfect for wheelchair users that enjoy exploring and spending time outdoors. There are hundreds of wheelchair-friendly trails across the city. One of the most attractive sides of Albuquerque is the fact that it has the warmest sun and freshest air in the region. Most of the public places in Albuquerque are 100% ADA compliant.

Accessible Travels

Accessible tourism enables people with access requirements, including mobility, vision, hearing and cognitive dimensions of access, to function independently and with equity and dignity through the delivery of universally designed tourism products, services, and environments.
